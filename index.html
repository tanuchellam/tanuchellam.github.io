A Little About Me

Entrepreneur, Leader, Traveler, Survivor

In 2012, a friend and I were crossing the road, when a truck crashed into us.

It changed my life forever.

So every day, I wake up wanting to make a difference in my short life. To me, meaning comes from giving back to society in a sustainable way while being able to provide for myself and my family. Technology is a tool, and a means for impacting change, and I believe in utilizing it to solve our challenges.




Professional Life

In the early 2000s, I created a startup designing websites for other small and medium businesses; I was an accidental entreprenuer at 14. Then, in 2010, I pivoted to help start a pro bono 501(c)(3) certified consultancy for social enterprises with some friends from Carnegie Mellon. Meanwhile, I've worked full time in product management and related roles in network security, fintech and pure tech, leading to a more general management role.



Based out of the San Francisco Bay Area, I am now at Autodesk leading the Product Delivery global team, while I continue to evangelize for my non-profit org as a hobby.

You'll find me constantly analyzing why products matter, are monetized and how they can be improved for more social good. I am a passionate advocate for diversity of thought, background, culture and am a mentor with professional organizations and programs like TechWomen.
